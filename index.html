DIY-Portfolio

A free and simple website template for makers who aren't afraid of a little html.

Applying to college? Or just need a simple website to showcase your work?

DIY-Portfolio is a free, downloadable website template that you can edit to create your own

personal project portfolio. It's open-source so you can keep all your changes.

How does it work?

.

You can download the template from this Github link. The website is written

with HTML, CSS, and Javascript/jQuery, and I've made everything

as simple as it can be. Once you have downloaded the template, that's it.

There are no more instructions. Open up the files in your text editor,

and figure things out.

.

The code is optimized for readability, and there are lots of comments to help you out.

As you get stronger at HTML, you will notice things that could be improved.

This is a good sign.

Why not just use Tumblr, or Wordpress?

.

That's a good question, and I think that you should absolutely use Tumblr or Wordpress if

you prefer how they look. They are also easier to use out of the box, no question.

.

But if you want a little more control, hate editing themes, or are just curious

about learning HTML, then DIY-Portfolio might be perfect for you.

Mess around.

.

Don't worry about breaking things. You can always undo the mistake.

Start by changing words you recognize. Refresh the browser and see what happens.

Google anything you don't understand.

I made DIY-Portfolio as a template for my own website.

Check it out to see a working example.

.

Why re-invent the wheel and make yet another website template?

.

That's a legitimate question, and I don't have an answer.

I learned to code by hacking other people's code. I only hope someone finds this useful.
